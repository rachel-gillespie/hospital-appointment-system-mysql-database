# Hospital Appointment System

---

## System Description

**Department:** Represents the different departments a hospital may have. Includes the attributes `departmentId`, `departmentName`, `location`, `floor`, and `phoneExtension`. `departmentId` is the primary key. A department can have many doctors.

**Doctor:** Represents the doctors that work at the hospital. Includes the attributes `doctorId`, `name` (firstName, lastName), `emailAddress`, `phoneNumber`, `specialisation`, `yearsOfExperience`, and `qualification`. `doctorId` is the primary key. A doctor may have one to two email addresses, one to three phone numbers, and one or more qualifications. A doctor can refer zero or more doctors and also be referred by zero or more doctors. A doctor can be part of one or more departments, be involved in zero or more appointments, and have zero or more patients.

**Patient:** Represents the patients that attend the hospital. Includes the attributes `patientId`, `name` (firstName, lastName), `dateOfBirth`, `gender`, `emailAddress`, `phoneNumber`, `bloodType`, and `registrationDate`. `patientId` is the primary key. A patient may have one to two email addresses and one to three phone numbers. A patient can attend zero or more appointments, be treated by one or more doctors, and have one or more medical records.

**Appointment:** Represents the appointments carried out in the hospital. Includes the attributes `appointmentId`, `date`, `time`, `status`, `reasonForVisit`, and `notes`. `appointmentId` is the primary key. An appointment can generate zero to one prescription and have one or more doctors involved.

**Prescription:** Represents the prescriptions generated by the appointments in the hospital. Includes the attributes `prescriptionId`, `medicationName`, `dosage`, `frequency`, `duration`, and `notes`. It is not a weak entity as it has its own identifier, but it exists only through an appointment.

**MedicalRecord:** Represents the medical records of every patient that attends the hospital. Includes the attributes `diagnosis`, `treatment`, and `date`. It is a weak entity that relies on `Patient(patientId)` as its primary key.

**InvolvedIn:** Represents the relationship between doctors and appointments. Includes the attribute `roles`, which records each doctor's specific role in a given appointment. A doctor can be involved in zero or more appointments and an appointment involves at least one doctor.

---

## Sample Queries/Transactions

The database should be able to handle or generate the following:

- Get all appointments for a specific patient, ordered by date.
- Get all doctors in a specific department.
- Get a patient's full medical history.
- List all appointments with the doctor and patient names involved in that appointment.
- Find all prescriptions for a specific patient.
- Count the number of appointments per doctor.
- Find all patients a specific doctor is involved with, including their role.
- Get all appointments scheduled within a date range.
- Find the most prescribed medication.
- List all doctors and their department name, ordered by department.

## Logical Data Model

### Department

```
Department(departmentId, departmentName, location, floor, phoneExtension)
```

- **Primary Key:** `departmentId`

---

### Doctor

```
Doctor(doctorId, firstName, lastName, specialisation, yearsOfExperience, departmentId)
```

- **Primary Key:** `doctorId`
- **Foreign Key:** `departmentId` → `Department(departmentId)`
  - On Update: Cascade
  - On Delete: Set Null

---
